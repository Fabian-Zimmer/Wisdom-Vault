## Ingredients

**Note:** Makes 4 balls/pitas.

| Amount           | Name                                               | Prep                                                                                      |
| ---------------- | -------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 125g             | White bread flour                                  |                                                                                           |
| 125g             | Whole wheat flour                                  |                                                                                           |
| 5g               | Salt                                               |                                                                                           |
| 2.5g / 3g / 7.5g | Instant dry yeast / active dry yeast / fresh yeast | For active dry yeast let it sit in the water for 10 min. before adding other ingredients. |
| 170g             | Cold water                                         |                                                                                           |
If flour substantially less than 13% protein content, hydration needs to be lowered by trial and error.

## Instructions

1. In a large bowl combine the water, yeast, and salt. Mix well to dissolve the salt and hydrate the yeast. Add the flours and mix to a dough.
2. Tip the dough out on the table and knead it for 4 minutes. (Desired dough temperature 25°C/77°F)
3. Divide the dough into 4 equal pieces and shape them into balls.
4. Place the dough balls on to an oiled plate and rub lightly with oil all over. I used olive oil.
5. Cover the dough balls and cold ferment them for 24 hours in the fridge.
6. Pre-heat your oven and baking tray/steel/stone to 250°C (480°F) fan off. The oven must be very hot, so heat it for at least 40 minutes.
	- Alternatively, use a pan to heat the pita, flipping frequently (each minute or so) until it puffs up.
7. Dust the dough ball generously with flour and roll it flat with a rolling pin. But do not make it too thin or else it will not rise well. Bake one at a time and adjust if need be. Also, note the orientation of the dough. It must face the same way as it was sitting on the plate.
8. Place the flattened dough onto the preheated tray and bake for 3–4 minutes.
