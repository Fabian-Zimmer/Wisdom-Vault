### Potato Gnocchi

| Amount | Name              | Prep                          |
| ------ | ----------------- | ----------------------------- |
| 600g   | Russet potatoes   | Peeled                        |
| 200g   | Semolina flour    | More like 250g if weighing potatoes uncooked |
| 1 tsp  | Salt              |                               |
|        | Rosemary          | Ground, optional              |
|        | Thyme             | Ground, optional              |

### Sauce (for half dough amount)

| Amount   | Name              | Prep         |
| -------- | ----------------- | ------------ |
| 50g      | Walnuts           | For blending |
| 100-125g | Ricotta cheese    |              |
| 250ml    | Plant-based cream | Soya cuisine |
| 1        | Garlic clove      |              |
| 1 tsp    | Agave             |              |
| 1/2 tsp  | Cinnamon          |              |
| 1/2 tsp  | Nutmeg            |              |
| ~10      | Sage leaves       | Finely cut   |
| Dashes   | Salt              | To taste     |
| Dashes   | Black pepper      | To taste     |

### Serving

| Amount | Name        | Prep            |
| ------ | ----------- | --------------- |
|        | Walnuts     | Roughly chopped |
|        | Parmesan    | Grated          |

## Instructions

1. Cut peeled russet potatoes (600g) into equal medium-sized pieces. Pressure cook for 1 whistle on high heat (8 on induction). The potatoes shouldn't be completely tender but should have a starchy dry consistency ("mehlig").
2. Meanwhile make the sauce, blend together using a staff mixer: walnuts (50g), ricotta cheese (100-125g), plant-based cream (250ml), garlic clove (1), agave (1 tsp), cinnamon (1/2 tsp), nutmeg (1/2 tsp), finely cut sage leaves (~10), and dashes of salt and pepper to taste.
3. Let the potatoes cool for a while, either before or after crushing.
4. Crush the potatoes into a paste with a fork or masher with holes. 
5. Spread some semolina flour on a large cutting/working board, add mashed potatoes, and add lots of semolina flour on top, also add salt (1 tsp), and optional ground rosemary and thyme. 
6. Knead into a large ball, adding rest of or more flour as necessary. The dough can be slightly gooey/sticky inside but not on the outside.
7. Cut the dough into 4 equal pieces. 1 piece is one large-ish portion (for 1-2 people, depending).
8. Roll 1 piece into long thick strips (work in two or more batches depending on board size, usually 2 batches necessary to get thin enough strips), then cut into (very) small equal pieces (if too thick, they won't cook properly and it's not tasty).
9. Cook the gnocchi in boiling water (optionally salted) until they float to the top (approximately 2-3 minutes), then let cook for approximately 1 minute while floating. Remove with a sieve or slotted spoon/ladle.
10. Heat up the sauce in wok or pot and add roughly chopped walnuts. Then add the cooked gnocchi and mix.
Top with grated parmesan.

**Note:** The sauce recipe is for half the gnocchi dough amount (i.e. 2 dough pieces).
