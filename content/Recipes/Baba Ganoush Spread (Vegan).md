## Ingredients

| Amount         | Name          | Prep                          |
| -------------- | ------------- | ----------------------------- |
| 2 medium-large | Eggplants     | Many small knife cuts on skin |
|                | Olive oil     | For rubbing                   |
|                | Salt          | For rubbing                   |
| 1 whole        | Garlic head   | Top cut off                   |
| 1/4 cup        | Tahini        |                               |
| 1              | Date          |                               |
| ~1/2           | Lemon/lime    | Juiced                        |
| 3 tbsp         | Olive oil     |                               |
| Handful        | Fresh parsley | Chopped                       |
| 5-6 turns      | Black pepper  | Freshly cracked               |
| 1/2 tsp        | Salt          |                               |
| 1/4 tsp        | Sumac         |                               |
| 1/4 tsp        | Cumin powder  |                               |
| Handful        | Pine nuts     |                               |

## Instructions

1. Make many small knife cuts all over the skin side of eggplants (2 medium-large). Rub with olive oil and salt.
2. Cut off the top of garlic head (1 whole), rub with olive oil, and place on baking tray with cut side exposed.
3. Place eggplants either: (a) flat side down directly on a clean baking tray, or (b) face down on aluminum foil. Bake at 210°C for 40 minutes alongside the garlic head.
   (I only tried method (b) so far, they will stick to aluminium paper quite a bit...)
4. If using aluminum foil method, flip eggplants at the end and roast for 2-3 minutes with top heating element on. But remove garlic head after 40 minutes, so they're not in this roasting phase.
5. Scoop out the eggplant flesh and blend/purée together with: roasted garlic, tahini (1/4 cup), date (1), lemon/lime juice (~1/2 lemon/lime), olive oil (3 tbsp), chopped fresh parsley (handful), freshly cracked black pepper (5-6 turns), salt (1/2 tsp), sumac (1/4 tsp), and cumin powder (1/4 tsp) until smooth.
6. Add pine nuts (handful) and pulse 2-3 times, leaving large bits of nuts.
