## Ingredients

| Amount     | Name                    | Prep                                  |
| ---------- | ----------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| 5-6        | Dried Shitake mushrooms |                                       |
| 3 cups     | Water                   | For soaking mushrooms                 |
| 2-3 sticks | Green/spring onions     | Finely chopped                        |
| 400g       | Cooked chickpeas        | Either from can, or soaked overnight  |
| ~500g      | Medium-firm tofu        | Cut into bite-sized cubes             |
| 4-5        | Garlic cloves           | Finely chopped                        |
|            | Ginger                  | Same volume as garlic, finely chopped |
| 2-3        | Green chillies          | Finely chopped                        |
| 2 tsp      | Sichuan peppercorns     | Toasted and ground                    |
| 3 tbsp     | Chili oil               |                                       |
| 150g       | Brown shimeji mushrooms |                                       |
| 1 tsp      | Cayenne pepper          | (optional)                            |
| 2 tbsp     | Doubanjiang             | "Sichuan Style Broad Bean Sauce"      |
| 1/2 tsp    | Brown sugar/jaggery     |                                       |
| 2 tsp      | Rice wine vinegar       |                                       |
| 1 tbsp     | Light soy sauce         |                                       |
| 1 tsp      | Dark soy sauce          |                                       |
| 2 tsp      | Potato starch           |                                       |
| 1 tbsp     | Water                   | For potato starch slurry              |

## Instructions

1. Add dried Shitake mushrooms (5-6) and water (3 cups) in a pot, cover, and bring to boil. Cook/simmer for approximately 10 minutes on low heat.
2. While mushrooms are cooking, finely chop green/spring onions (2-3 sticks).
3. Mash cooked chickpeas (400g either from can or soaked overnight) with a fork to get crumbles.
4. Cut medium-firm tofu (~500g) into bite-sized cubes.
5. Fish out cooked mushrooms and set aside, saving the cooking water. Cool mushrooms with cold water, then squeeze by hand to remove excess water. Cut away stems and finely chop the caps.
6. Finely chop garlic (4-5 cloves), ginger (same volume as garlic), and green chillies (2-3).
7. Toast Sichuan peppercorns (2 tsp) in a wok for approximately 1 minute, then grind in a mortar.
8. In same wok, heat chili oil (3 tbsp), sauté ginger, garlic, green chillies, and spring onions for ~3-5 minutes.
9. Add crumbled chickpeas, chopped Shitake mushrooms, and brown shimeji mushrooms (150g), and cook for ~5-8 minutes.
10. Add cayenne pepper (1 tsp, optional), doubanjiang (2 tbsp), toasted and ground Sichuan peppercorns, the reserved mushroom water, brown sugar/jaggery (1/2 tsp), rice wine vinegar (2 tsp), light soy sauce (1 tbsp), and dark soy sauce (1 tsp). Cover wok with lid and let simmer for ~5 minutes.
11. Add tofu cubes (~500g) and let simmer with lid for approximately ~10 minutes.
12. Mix potato starch (2 tsp) and water (1 tbsp) into a slurry, then add to the wok to thicken the sauce.
