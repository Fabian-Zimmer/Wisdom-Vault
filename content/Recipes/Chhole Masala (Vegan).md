## Ingredients

| 2 big (~200g)         | Tomatoes      | roughly chopped                 |

## Instructions

**Note:** Make rice or rotis for serving.

0. (if not already done): soak chickpeas (2 cups) overnight, or 8h minimum.
1. Pressure cook chickpeas with salt (1 tsp), chai powder (1 tsp), and water so that it is just covered. Cook on high heat (~9 on induction) and once cooker is warm reduce to medium heat (~5 on induction) for 3 whistles.
2. Meanwhile, roughly chop red onions (2 medium-sized), tomatoes (2 big | ~200g), garlic (3-4 cloves), and ginger (same volume as garlic). Then cook all in a pot for a few minutes.
3. Take the pot off heat and puree directly in the pot with a staff mixer.
4. In a wok, add black cardamom (2-3 pieces), green cardamom pods (4-5 pieces), cinnamon stick (thick piece), bay leaves (2-3 pieces), green chillies (3 chopped), then heat until fragrant.
5. Add the purée to the wok (careful it may splash if heat is too high), and only as much such that the chickpeas are the dominant part of the meal (set aside rest of gravy if too much).
6. Add Chhole masala (3 tsp, then more to taste), haldi powder (1 tsp), and salt to taste.
7. Mix in chopped coriander leaves.
8. Add water for desired consistency.
9. Serve with rice or rotis.
